Public School

Opting for public education, Oliver finds himself within the walls of Waterford Secondary School.
The school has outdated infrastructure and overcrowded classrooms, with peeling paint and worn-out desks.
Situated in the heart of the working-class neighborhood, the school stands as a symbol of the challenges that students from disadvantaged backgrounds face.
The lack of resources and overcrowding hinder Oliver's academic development and affect his self-esteem.
