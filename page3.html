Privat school

Oliver gets offered to a secure place in a prestigious private school at Eton high school because of a limited event for poor people, and the world may appear to open up before him.
Surrounded by affluence and privilege, he is swept into an unknown place of exclusivity and entitlement.
Yet, as Oliver settles into this new environment, he finds himself grappling with a different set of challenges.
The pressure to conform, the weight of expectations, and the glaring inequality within the school gates threaten to suffocate his spirit.
Despite the advantages, Oliver realizes that the social divisions perpetuated by private education negate the principles of equal opportunities.
